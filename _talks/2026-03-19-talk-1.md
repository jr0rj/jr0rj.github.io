Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) is a widely used method in Bayesian Inference but can be difficult to implement effectively due to complex posterior density functions and identifiability issues. This talk provides a practical walkthrough of the random walk Metropolis-Hastings algorithm demonstrated by the fitting of an SIR model to infection data. 
 
We begin with a 'naive' walk that does not effectively explore the parameter space. By considering reparameterisations, convergence statistics and acceptance rates, I will show how a RWMH algorithm can be diagnosed and made much more efficient. This talk will prioritise visuals over dense mathematics including (if I have time) some animated trace and spaghetti plots to show exactly how the RWMH algorithm explores the posterior.
